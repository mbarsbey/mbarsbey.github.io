I am a computer science PhD student in **Boğaziçi University**, with a research focus on **<span style="color: #d68300">robust generalization</span>** in **<span style="color: #d68300">deep learning</span>** and **<span style="color: #d68300">probabilistic latent variable models</span>**. I have conducted research in a wide range of topics including compressibility in neural networks, causal discovery, human-AI complementarity, robust drug-target affinity predictions, and approximate inference in latent variable models. Last summer I was a research scientist intern in **DeepMind London**, working on **learning to defer** to experts based on ML model predictions, as well as learning from multiple experts with **noisy annotations**.

I have **industry experience** on research and deployment of machine learning systems, both as a team lead and freelance consultant, involving work on **multivariate time series** analysis, **AutoML** system development, **representation learning** of users among others. At the height of my involvement in the industry, I was in charge of the team that oversaw the forecasting of withdrawals from **8000 ATMs** daily.

I have a BA and MA in psychology from my alma mater, where I mainly focused on **psycholoinguistics** research, as well as experiment design and statistical inference. My thesis was on **embodied language processing**, investigating how understanding long texts involved perceptual representations. In my research visit to **UC Berkeley's** Concepts and Cognition Lab, we conducted research into how different word choices can affect **causal explanation** tendencies.
