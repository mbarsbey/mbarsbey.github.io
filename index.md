I am a machine learning researcher with a focus on **<span style="color: #d68300">compression</span>** and **<span style="color: #d68300">robust generalization</span>**. I am currently a post-doc at **<span style="color: #d600be">Imperial College London</span>** (Note: Our paper on compressibility and robustness to spurious correlations have been accepted to **<span style="color: blue">ICCV 2025</span>** - link coming soon!). Before this, I was a research scientist intern at **<span style="color: #0053d6">DeepMind London</span>**, working on robust **<span style="color: #0053d6">human-AI complementarity</span>** ([<span style="color: blue">published in Nature Medicine</span>](https://www.nature.com/aricles/s41591-023-02437-x)), as well as robust evaluation and training under **<span style="color: #0053d6">uncertain ground truth</span>** ([<span style="color: blue">published in Medical Image Analysis</span>](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1361841525001033)). I have completed my PhD in **<span style="color: #d600be">Boğaziçi University</span>**, with a research focus on **<span style="color: #d68300">robust generalization</span>** in **<span style="color: #d68300">deep learning</span>** and **<span style="color: #d68300">probabilistic latent variable models</span>**.

I have **<span style="color: #d600be">industry experience</span>** on research and deployment of machine learning systems, both as a team lead and freelance consultant, involving work on **<span style="color: #d68300">multivariate time series</span>** analysis, **<span style="color: #d68300">AutoML</span>** system development, and **<span style="color: #d68300">predictive maintenance</span>** among others. At the height of my involvement in the industry, I was in charge of the team that oversaw the forecasting of withdrawals from **<span style="color: #0053d6">8000 ATMs</span>** daily.

I have a BA and MA in **<span style="color: #d600be">psychology</span>** from my alma mater, where I mainly focused on **<span style="color: #d600be">psycholinguistics</span>** research, as well as experiment design and statistical inference. My thesis was on **<span style="color: #d68300">embodied language processing</span>**, investigating how understanding long texts utilized perceptual representations. In my research visit to UC Berkeley's **<span style="color: #0053d6">Concepts and Cognition Lab</span>**, I investigated how different wording choices a language affords can affect a person's **<span style="color: #0053d6">causal explanations</span>**.
