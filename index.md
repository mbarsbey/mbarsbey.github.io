I am a computer science PhD student in **<span style="color: #d600be">Boğaziçi University</span>**, with a research focus on **<span style="color: #d68300">robust generalization</span>** in **<span style="color: #d68300">deep learning</span>** and **<span style="color: #d68300">probabilistic latent variable models</span>**. I have conducted research in a wide range of topics including [compressibility in neural networks](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper/2021/hash/f5c3dd7514bf620a1b85450d2ae374b1-Abstract.html), [causal discovery](https://openreview.net/forum?id=ByeB5k2Etr), [human-AI complementarity](https://www.researchsquare.com/article/rs-2231672/v1), [robust drug-target affinity predictions](http://recomb2023.bilkent.edu.tr/program.html), and [approximate inference in latent variable models](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9296951). In Summer 2022, I was a research scientist intern at **<span style="color: #0053d6">DeepMind London</span>**, working on **<span style="color: #0053d6">learning to defer</span>** to experts based on ML model predictions, as well as learning from multiple experts with **<span style="color: #0053d6">noisy annotations</span>**.

I have **<span style="color: #d600be">industry experience</span>** on research and deployment of machine learning systems, both as a team lead and freelance consultant, involving work on **<span style="color: #d68300">multivariate time series</span>** analysis, **<span style="color: #d68300">AutoML</span>** system development, and **<span style="color: #d68300">predictive maintenance</span>** among others. At the height of my involvement in the industry, I was in charge of the team that oversaw the forecasting of withdrawals from **<span style="color: #0053d6">8000 ATMs</span>** daily.

I have a BA and MA in **<span style="color: #d600be">psychology</span>** from my alma mater, where I mainly focused on psycholinguistics research, as well as experiment design and statistical inference. My thesis was on **<span style="color: #d68300">embodied language processing</span>**, investigating how understanding long texts utilized perceptual representations. In my research visit to UC Berkeley's **<span style="color: #0053d6">Concepts and Cognition Lab</span>**, I investigated how different wording choices a language affords can affect a person's **<span style="color: #0053d6">causal explanations</span>**.
